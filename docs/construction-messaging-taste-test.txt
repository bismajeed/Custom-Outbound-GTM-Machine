===============================================================================
SYNTHESIZED TASTE PROFILE  (the rules to write by)
===============================================================================

SUBJECTS
  - Concrete, provocative-but-RESPECTFUL waste reframes. Point at the system/
    process, NEVER insult the people.
  - No-signal lane:  "you're paying estimators to copy-paste",
                     "data entry is eating your precon team",
                     "your estimators are doing data entry",
                     "stop paying estimators to copy-paste"
  - Curiosity lane:  "the part of bidding nobody fixes",
                     "is this slowing your bids down too?"
  - Signal lane (tool known): name the tool as the culprit —
                     "Procore's costing your estimators hours"
  - Format: lowercase, mid-length (NOT ultra-short 2-word); company-named
    questions OK; sentence-case OK.
  - BANNED: "free", "pilot", flat labels ("rand* precon backlog"), cutesy
    ("bidding's dumbest hour"), vague teasers, loss-aversion, em-dashes.

OPENING LINES
  - Blunt half-truth / number-anchored / direct question. Concrete > empathy.
  - e.g. "Half of what your estimators do all day isn't estimating, it's
    re-typing." / "How much of your estimators' week is actual estimating vs.
    data entry?"

BODY
  - Ruthlessly short: hook + ONE line on the mechanism (how we pull the data) +
    ask. No fabricated metrics.
  - Greeting: "{first_name} -"
  - CTA lives in a P.S.: "P.S. want me to run it on one of your recent bids?"

DELIVERABILITY
  - Plain ASCII punctuation (no em-dashes/curly quotes). Avoid spam words.
